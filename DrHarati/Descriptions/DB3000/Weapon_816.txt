OVERVIEW: The AGM-84L Harpoon Block II is an air launched, turbo-jet powered, solid-fuel, sea-skimming, long range, radar guided anti-ship/anti-surface missile equipped with a 221 kilogram warhead.

DETAILS: The AGM-84L Harpoon Block II is a major upgrade to the Harpoon, giving it an anti-surface capability. The missile incorporates an inertial measuring unit and a GPS receiver, giving the missile a land attack capability.
 
	A typical ASUW engagement by an aircraft begins with the launching aircraft being cued to the target position either through onboard sensors, or via reporting from other targeting platforms using passive or active means.  The launching aircraft will then typically approach the target from below the radar horizon.  Prior to launch, the radar is programed for a large, medium or small acquisition window.  Although a smaller acquisition window requires more precise  
targeting data, it also minimizes target warning time. Prior to launch, the Harpoon is also programmed to one of two modes: The Range and Bearing Launch (RBL) or the Bearing Only mode. In the RBL mode, the AGM-84L Harpoon descends to a low cruise altitude immediately after launch, using its radar altimeter and inertial navigation to guide it to a preselected point near the calculated target position.  It then activates its frequency-agile, active J-band (10 to 20 GHz) radar seeker to guide it to the target.  In the Bearing Only launch mode, the Harpoon is launched down a target bearing and the radar seeker is activated shortly after launch.  If no suitable target is found, the Harpoon switches to a search pattern and self-destructs if it fails to acquire a target.

	In either mode, once the seeker acquires a target, the missile enters the terminal attack phase, approaching to approximately 1.5 km from the target before executing a "pop-up" maneuver that terminates with a dive into the target.

Specifications:

Speed:	0.85 mach
Range:	222 km
Weight: 544 kg
 

NOTES: IOC 2003. The Block II is can be retro-fitted to existing Harpoon missiles.
