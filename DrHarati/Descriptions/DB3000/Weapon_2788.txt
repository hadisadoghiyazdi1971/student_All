OVERVIEW:  The Spike NLOS (Non Line of Sight) aka "Tamuz" is a solid fuel, "fire and forget" anti-vehicle/ground attack missile using a dual-mode electro-optical (EO)/infrared (IR) seeker. It can be equipped with a variety of warheads. 

DETAILS:   The Spike NLOS can be operated in either direct attack mode or midcourse navigation mode based on target coordinates, providing the capability to engage targets at stand-off range with no line of sight.  Its dual mode seeker allows for target engagement in reduced visibility or night conditions.  The Spike-NLOS TV camera and datalink allow the operator to select a wide-angle view during its flight, giving the missile a limited reconnaissance capability.  The TV guidance and datalink also provides a "Lock-On After Launch" (LOAL) aka “Fire-and-Observe” capability, giving the operator the ability to maneuver, retarget or even abort the missile in flight. The system can also tie into a C3 network, utilizing data from other sources such as counterbattery radars and drones to direct the missile to its target.
