OVERVIEW:  The RB-70 Bolide platoon is a short-range, air defense unit using the RBS-70 laser beam riding A/D missile.

DETAILS: The RB 70 missile uses a laser beam detector mounted at the back of the missile which detects the laser guidance beam. The RB 70 uses an adaptable proximity fuse that provides all-target capability and a high kill probability against small targets such as UAVs and missiles.

It can be operated independently in stand-alone mode or configured as part of an anti-aircraft battery linked with the Giraffe land mobile surveillance radar. The RBS 70 is equipped with a close loop cooled Clip-On Night Device (COND), giving the system day and night capability.


Specifications:
 
Maximum Acquisition Range: 	variable, depending on AD sensor integration.
Maximum Tracking Range:		4 nm (laser designator)
Engagement Envelope:		250m-8 km (range); 9-5,000 m (altitude)
Reload time:			7 seconds 
Mobility:			Man portable. Approx. 30 seconds deployment time  

NOTES:  IOC 2005. Because of the requirement to keep the laser beam on target, troops employing the RBS 70 require considerably more training than required to operate most infrared-guided SAMs. Additionally, it is difficult to guide a SACLOS missile from a moving platform.
