OVERVIEW: The GBU-27B/B Paveway III is a laser and GPS guided bomb (LGB).


DETAILS: The GBU-27B/B Paveway III is intended for precision bombing against hardened targets.  Intended for low-level delivery, it uses a mid-course autopilot and a more sensitive seeker than earlier Paveway I and II.  The Paveway III consists of a bolt-on gimballed seeker head and Computer Control Group (CCG) and a stabilizing tail airfoil assembly attached to a conventional BLU-116/B penetrating bomb.   This converts the BLU-116/B into a laser-guided weapon that homes in on a reflected laser beam transmitted from a ground or air-based laser designator.  Unlike the earlier Paveway I and II series, the  Paveway III-series bomb uses a microprocessor to provide proportional control to the control surfaces, giving smaller control surface deflections and a smoother flight path, resulting in a significant increase in range over its predecessors. Reported accuracy is 1 meter CEP. The GPS receiver allows employment of the GBU-27B/B in all weather.  The BLU-116/B warhead is capable of penetrating 8-12 feet of reinforced concrete.


NOTES:  The GBU-27 is a GBU-24 modified for delievery by the F-117.  First combat use in 2003.
