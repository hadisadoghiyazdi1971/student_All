OVERVIEW:  The SA-6b GAINFUL mod 1 (2K12 KUB; 9M336) is a mobile, two-stage, solid fuel rocket and ramjet powered, low-to-medium altitude, surface-to-air missile  using command guidance with terminal semi-active radar homing (SARH).  It is equipped with a 57 kg warhead.

DETAILS:  The SA-6 GAINFUL was designed to supplement the larger and longer ranging 2K11 Krug / SA-4 Ganef with  low altitude coverage air defense, and was primarily intended to protect front line-armor and mechanized divisions.  The SA-6b features enhanced capability against jamming.


Specifications:
 
Weight: 		630 kg 
Length: 		5.80 m (with booster)
Diameter: 		0.335 m (missile), 0.65 (booster)
Wingspan: 		1.245 m (booster)
Speed:			600 m/s
Range:			3.2-11.9 nm (6-22 km)
Engagement Altitude:	65-26,250 ft (100-7000 m)   
Reload time:		10 minutes (approx.)
Max target speed: 	1166 knts (2,159 km/h)
Kill probability:	0.7 (single missile v. fighter-type aircraft)
Maneuverability:	15g	


NOTES:  IOC:  1976. Reported to be able to defeat an 8g target.
