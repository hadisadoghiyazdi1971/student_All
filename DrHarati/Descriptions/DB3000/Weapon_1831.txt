OVERVIEW:  The SA-10b GRUMBLE (5V55R) aka GRUMBLE mod 1 is a single stage, solid fuel, long-range, TVM guided  surface-to-air missile.  It has a 133 kg warhead utilizing a RF proximity or impact fuze.

DETAILS: The SA-10b series was developed as an area defense/strategic SAM system for both land and naval use. It uses Track via Missile (TVM) for guidance. The TVM guidance requires target illumination from a ground station and reception of the reflected signal by the missile, which relays it back to the ground station via a datalink.  The ground station then computes an interception course and uses the datalink to send guidance commands back to the missile.  The missile uses a thrust vectoring system for enhanced maneuverability.

NOTES:  IOC 1982.  The 5V55R is part of the S-300PS self-propelled system.  To ensure missile reliability, the missile uses the TPK combined launch and transport container. This container was never opened until missile launch. The vertical launch technique adopted used a catapult accelerator within the container to pop the missile to an altitude 20 m above the launcher, where the main motor ignited.
