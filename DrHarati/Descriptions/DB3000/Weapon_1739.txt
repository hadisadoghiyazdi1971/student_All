OVERVIEW: The AS.34 Komoran 2 is an air-launched, solid fuel, radar homing, sea-skimming, anti-ship missile with a secondary land-attack capability.

DETAILS:  The Kormoran was designed specifically for launch by low flying aircraft using pop up tactics. The missile has four firing modes: Silent/Missile Acquisition, Radar, Visual and Offset. After launch and completion of the boost phase, the AS.34 Komoran 2 descends to a cruise altitude of 20 meters, using an inertial guidance system for the midcourse phase. When within line-of-sight of the target, the missile begins scanning for target emissions.  If signals are picked up, the homing head locks on to the emitter and the missile operates in a passive homing mode until 15-20 seconds prior to impact in order to evade detection and limit reaction time. If no signals are detected, the enters active radar homing mode. After locking on to a target, the missile descends to its final sea-skimming flight level to hit the target just above the water line. It carries a 220 kg delay-fused warhead, designed for 90mm of penetration prior to detonation. The Kormoran 2 incorporates more launch modes, improves discrimination facilitating target selection and acquisition as well as greater electronic countermeasures resistance over the earlier Komoran 1.  Unlike the Komoran 1, the addition of a more powerful booster engine allows the Koroman 2 to be launched from slower aircraft and helicopters.
