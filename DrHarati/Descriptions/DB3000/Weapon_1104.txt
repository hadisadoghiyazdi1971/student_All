OVERVIEW: The AIM-9G Sidewinder is a short-range, supersonic, infra-red guided (IR) air-to-air missile with a 9 kilogram warhead.   

DETAILS: The AIM-9G was essentially a -9D with one key improvement-the Sidewinder Expanded Acquisition Mode (SEAM). The seeker was slaved to the aircraft's acquisition radar, and it could revolve 25 degrees in a circular scan, instead of being aimed straight ahead. This 'off boresight' capability improved the odds of acquiring targets. The 9G also used solid-state modules. Like earlier Sidewinders, the AIM-9G Sidewinder is capable of only rear-aspect attacks.   The AIM-9G also featured a larger 9-kilogram continuous rod warhead providing a fragmentation effect.
