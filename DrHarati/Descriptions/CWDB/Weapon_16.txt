OVERVIEW:  The SA-3b GOA mod 1 (S-125 Neva, 5V27b, V-601P) is a two-stage, solid-fuel, short range, low-to-medium altitude, radio command guidance, surface to air missile equipped with a 72 kg warhead using proximity,  radar and contact fuzing.

DETAILS:  The SA-3b GOA was designed as a low-to-medium altitude complement to the medium-to-high altitude SA-2 system.  After launch and booster jettison, the second stage is captured in the radar beam and guidance signals are sent to place the missile on an intercept trajectory.  The missile has a lethal radius of over 12.5 m. 

Specifications: 
Weight: 		953 kg 
Length: 		5.95 m (with booster)
Diameter: 		0.39 m (missile), 0.55 m (booster)
Wingspan: 		1.22 m
Speed:			3.5 mach
Range:			2.2-13.5 nm (4-25 km)
Altitude		656-45,935 feet (200-14,000 m)
Reload time:		50 minutes
Max target speed: 	700 km/h (head on); 300 km/h (receding)
Max Manuverability:	6 g
